One of A Kind:  
7 Poems by Justin Nguyen-Nguyen.

The first thing that grabbed my attention was the colour scheme of the website. The website is covered in different muted, desaturated green shades. It provokes a sombre, melancholy, nostalgic, yet soothing feeling. It also reminds me of a sense of decay, like dying leaves on a lonely branch.

Observing
I observed the layout and design of the website. Similar to question 1, I think the website’s green colour theme portrays a sombre mood. The design is simple and reminds me of 2000s websites. The font used on the website is Arial. There are dark green boxes under random sets of words, containing looped sets of words and signs.
Clicking and hovering mouse
When I click on a box, a set of words appears along with certain music notes. When I click on the same box again, the box stops changing words, the color between the box and the word swaps, the note elongates for a bit and stops like pressing on a piano key. If I press the name of the poem on the top left, all the notes reset. If I hover to the X on the bottom right, the X rotates, and a blue list appears along with different poems and poets. Clicking mute mutes all sounds.
Listening
If I leave the box shuffling words, the word loops and the music loops also. Each box loops one note that plays every time a word changes. If I pause all the boxes, the music stops. I think this is note-worthy because I thought something would happen if I paused all the boxes. The boxes, in the order from left to right, play notes a pitch higher than its ascendant.

Most of my time was spent interacting with all the boxes, as they are the main components of the website.

My most common action was clicking. I thought a simple and vague website like this would contain some kind of secret, so I clicked everywhere I could.

While the website itself is simple, there is much more to meet the eye and requires visitors to pay attention to a lot of details. I believe it is meant to convey a story; however, I haven’t found out what the story is yet. I believe the author is trying to incorporate feelings with seemingly simple, boring, structural words using the versatility and emotion of music and colour. Words from each box combined with quotation marks suggest a conversation being told. I believe it is a story because the author is Vietnamese (Nguyen is a Vietnamese surname), and Ocean Vuong is a Vietnamese American poet and novelist. Ocean Vuong is also a character in Vietnamese folklore. The website also references the Lovefeast, which is a meal eaten in common by a Christian congregation in token of brotherly.

I need to guess the goal of the website and I’m not really sure about it, so I don’t think the goals of the website was well communicated. There was no guide or explanation for the website either, just going straight into interacting. However, after some research, I have learnt that Tiger Dingsun – the author of the website – stated that he looked for non-linear text and poems to create the websites, which might be the reason why the content of the website seems confusing.

Personally, I would visit the website for 3 or 4 times, about 5 minutes each. There is not much content to interact with. The first time would be to get used to the website and see what it could do, but the function of the website and the intentions of the author would intrigue visitors for a second visit. On the second visit, visitors experiment more with the boxes, possibly find out about the sequence of melodies and words and come up with theories on the author’s purposes. However, it is common for simply designed yet dubious websites like this to contain some kind of secret, which leads to the third and final visit: to explore the secrets. I think it’s similar to exploring a game: getting used to the functions, then playing through the game, then finally finishing the Easter Eggs.

The website clearly portrays that it should only be interacted with in a short period of time of a few visits. The simplicity of the website suggests that the website contains little amount of content. The words and melodies might seem random at first, but if paid enough attention, it is quite easy to see a pattern. The website provides a short, simple yet intriguing experience that can be explored in a short amount of time, without requiring repeated visits.

Another media form the experience references is music.

The references suggest interacting with the boxes. Every box contains one musical note that plays every time the word changes. It also suggests the visitors doing some testing and thinking. I believe the author expects the readers to play around with the boxes and create their own melodies through different combinations of boxes.

I believe the author wants to provoke intrigued yet confused feelings from the visitors. The user clicks on a box and is met with music, thus wondering “What am I going to hear when I click on other boxes?”, and “What is the purpose of the music?”. Then, the user interacts with all the boxes, and figures out the way the pitch of the notes gets higher in accordance to the order of the boxes. It’s a small, yet exciting “aha” moment nonetheless.

The most frustrating element of the interaction to me is the box. It’s very much a personal thing. When I first opened the website and saw such simple layout, I was expecting some surprises behind the little green boxes, but all they do are looping words and playing one music note. I spent a little while trying to figure out if I was missing something, and that was what made it so frustrating.

The most satisfying element for me is the content inside the boxes. On my first interaction with the website, I thought it was just random words and noises, but the more I looked into it, I found out that the random sounds are just notes at different pitches, and the words with quotation marks suggest conversations, and I realize that this website is trying to communicating a story. It is like finding a treasure on an island for me, although nowhere near as exciting, but fundamentally it is still the same feeling.
